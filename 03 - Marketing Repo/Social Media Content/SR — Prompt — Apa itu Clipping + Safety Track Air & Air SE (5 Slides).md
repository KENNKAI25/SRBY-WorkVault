## ═══ PROMPT ═══

**Reference:** Use the Saramonic design system. All product data and feature details in this prompt are verified — do not modify.

Create a **5-slide static image carousel** for Instagram (1080 × 1350 px, 4:5) and TikTok (1080 × 1920 px, 9:16). Deliver both formats for each slide.

---

### SERIES OVERVIEW

**Topic:** Audio clipping — what it is, why it ruins content, and how to prevent it using the Safety Track feature on Saramonic Air & Air SE
**Content arc:** Hook (Slide 1) → explain problem (Slides 2–3) → introduce solution (Slide 4) → how-to (Slide 5)
**Tone:** Direct, practical, creator-first. Not a physics lecture — explain it the way a sound engineer would to a creator who just ruined a take.
**Visual language:** Abstract/concept-driven Slides 1–3. Product + UI-focused Slides 4–5.

---

### SLIDE 1 — HOOK

**Goal:** Make anyone who's experienced clipping immediately stop scrolling.

**Layout:**
- Top: Series label tag — small, uppercase — `AUDIO PROBLEM`
- Center: Strong visual representing audio clipping — a waveform hitting the ceiling/ceiling limit and getting cut off flat, or a distortion graphic. The visual concept: sound that's too loud to be captured correctly.
- Bottom: Headline text block

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Pernah rekaman bagus, tapi audionya cempreng dan distorsi?`
- Subhead: `Itu clipping. Dan ada cara mencegahnya.`

**Visual notes:**
- No product on this slide — concept only
- The waveform visual must clearly show the "flat top" clipping shape — this is the key visual metaphor for the whole series
- Energy: relatable frustration, not alarming
- Saramonic logo bottom-right, small

---

### SLIDE 2 — WHAT IS CLIPPING?

**Goal:** Define clipping clearly. One concept, one visual.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `01` / `APA ITU CLIPPING?`
- Center: Waveform diagram showing the difference — normal waveform (peaks rounded, within range) vs. clipped waveform (peaks cut flat at the limit). Label both clearly.
- Bottom: Definition text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Clipping = suara yang terpotong.`
- Body: `Terjadi saat volume input terlalu tinggi untuk direkam mic. Puncak gelombang suara terpotong — hasilnya distorsi yang tidak bisa diperbaiki di editing.`

**Visual notes:**
- Two-panel waveform diagram: left = healthy signal, right = clipped signal
- The "flat top" on the clipped waveform must be visually obvious
- Label both panels — `NORMAL` and `CLIPPING`
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 3 — IMPACT ON CONTENT

**Goal:** Make the consequence personal and concrete for creators.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `02` / `DAMPAKNYA?`
- Center: Creator scenario visual — handheld interview, loud event, or outdoor shoot — with a visual signal distortion overlay representing the problem. Conveys: loud environment + mic = clipping risk.
- Bottom: Explanation text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Audio yang clipping tidak bisa diselamatkan.`
- Body: `Berbeda dengan noise yang bisa dikurangi di post-pro, distorsi dari clipping bersifat permanen. Satu take yang clipping = take ulang atau take yang terbuang.`

**Visual notes:**
- Scene should feel like a real creator situation with high-volume audio risk: concert, event, interview in noisy space
- Distortion/clipping represented as a visual waveform element overlaid on the scene — not literal
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 4 — SOLUTION: Safety Track Feature

**Goal:** Introduce the Safety Track feature as the direct answer to the clipping problem. Build desire before the how-to.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `03` / `SOLUSINYA?`
- Center: Feature name displayed prominently as a graphic element — `SAFETY TRACK` — with a simple diagram showing the concept: two parallel recording channels, one at normal level, one at a lower backup level
- Bottom: Feature explanation text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Safety Track.`
- Body: `Rekam dua track sekaligus — satu di level normal, satu di level backup yang lebih rendah. Kalau track utama clipping, track backup menyelamatkan rekaman kamu.`
- Tag line below: `Tersedia di Saramonic Air & Air SE.`

**Visual notes:**
- `SAFETY TRACK` as the visual centerpiece — treat it like a product feature badge or call-out graphic
- Diagram: two parallel waveform tracks — top = normal level (with clipping risk shown), bottom = backup level (clean, within range)
- No product photo yet — keep focus on the concept
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 5 — HOW TO: Enable Safety Track

**Goal:** Practical instruction. Show exactly where to enable it on each product. Clear, scannable, no ambiguity.

**Layout:**
- Top: Slide label — `04` / `CARA AKTIFKAN SAFETY TRACK`
- Center: **Split panel layout** — two equal columns side by side
  - **Left panel:** Saramonic Air — product image + instruction
  - **Right panel:** Saramonic Air SE — product image + instruction
- A thin divider line between panels
- Bottom: Closing line

**On-image copy — exact copy, do not change:**

Left panel:
- Product label: `Saramonic Air`
- Step: `Aktifkan di pengaturan receiver (RX).`

Right panel:
- Product label: `Saramonic Air SE`
- Step: `Aktifkan di pengaturan aplikasi.`

Bottom closing line: `Satu setting. Rekaman aman.`

**Visual notes:**
- Both product images must be present — Air on the left, Air SE on the right
- Panel size equal — no product gets more visual weight than the other
- Instructions must be short enough to read in 3 seconds — this is a quick-reference slide
- The split layout must feel balanced and intentional, not cramped
- Saramonic logo bottom-right, outside the split panels

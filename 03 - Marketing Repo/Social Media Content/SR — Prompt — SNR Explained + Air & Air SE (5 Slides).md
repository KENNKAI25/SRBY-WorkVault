## ═══ PROMPT ═══

**Reference:** Use the Saramonic design system. All product data in this prompt is verified — do not modify specs.

Create a **5-slide static image carousel** for Instagram (1080 × 1350 px, 4:5) and TikTok (1080 × 1920 px, 9:16). Deliver both formats for each slide.

---

### SERIES OVERVIEW

**Topic:** Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) — what it is, why it matters for content creators, and why Saramonic Air & Air SE leads on this spec
**Content arc:** Hook → explain concept (Slides 1–2) → connect to creator pain point (Slides 3–4) → product reveal with hard numbers (Slide 5)
**Tone:** Confident, educational, creator-first — like a senior creator explaining gear to a friend, not a spec sheet read-out
**Visual language:** Concept-driven Slides 1–4 (waveform/signal graphics, no product). Hard product reveal on Slide 5 with numbers front and center.

---

### SLIDE 1 — COVER / HOOK

**Goal:** Stop the scroll with a relatable pain point. Make the audience feel seen before you explain anything.

**Layout:**
- Top: Series label tag — small, uppercase — `AUDIO 101`
- Center: Bold visual representing bad audio — degraded waveform, static noise graphic, or visual "dirty signal" concept
- Bottom: Headline text block

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Noise audio kamu parah?`
- Subhead: `Kenalan sama satu angka yang menentukan segalanya.`

**Visual notes:**
- No product on this slide — concept only
- Visual should immediately communicate "bad audio / noise problem"
- Energy: urgent but not panicky — pro-friendly, not clickbait-y
- Saramonic logo bottom-right, small

---

### SLIDE 2 — CONCEPT: What is SNR?

**Goal:** Define Signal-to-Noise Ratio simply. One idea, one visual.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `01` / `SIGNAL TO NOISE RATIO`
- Center: Split diagram — left side labeled "SIGNAL" (clean waveform), right side labeled "NOISE" (messy/degraded waveform). Simple, clear, graphic.
- Bottom: Definition text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)`
- Body: `Perbandingan antara suara yang kamu mau rekam vs. noise yang ikut terekam. Makin tinggi angkanya, makin bersih audionya.`

**Visual notes:**
- Diagram must be simple and readable at thumbnail size — 2 columns, clear labels
- Use contrasting visual treatment: clean signal = sharp/clear, noise = distorted/rough
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 3 — CONCEPT BRIDGE: SNR in real numbers

**Goal:** Make the abstract concept concrete. Give the audience a mental anchor for what "high" vs "low" SNR means.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `02` / `ARTINYA?`
- Center: Simple scale or comparison graphic — e.g. two vertical bars or a gauge showing LOW SNR vs HIGH SNR with dB values
- Bottom: Explanation text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Bedanya SNR rendah vs. tinggi.`
- Body: `SNR rendah = noise masuk ke rekaman. SNR tinggi = suara kamu yang dominan, noise tersisih.`

**Visual notes:**
- Visual: two-bar comparison or simple spectrum graphic — LOW (labeled, degraded look) vs HIGH (labeled, clean look)
- Keep it graphic/abstract — no product yet
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 4 — CREATOR PAIN POINT: How SNR affects your content

**Goal:** Land the "so what" for content creators. Connect SNR to real recording scenarios they've experienced.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `03` / `KENAPA PENTING?`
- Center: Use-case visual — creator recording scenario (interview, outdoor vlog, handheld setup) with a visual noise interference element overlaid — representing the problem
- Bottom: Explanation text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `SNR rendah = konten kamu kalah sama background.`
- Body: `Outdoor shoot, keramaian, atau AC ruangan — mic dengan SNR rendah rekam semua itu. Hasilnya: post-production jadi kerja dua kali.`

**Visual notes:**
- Creator scenario should feel familiar: outdoor street, interview setup, or indoor with ambient noise
- Noise interference can be represented as a visual overlay or waveform distortion — not literal
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 5 — PRODUCT REVEAL: Saramonic Air & Air SE

**Goal:** Hard product reveal. Lead with the number, anchor to competitors, close with brand.

**Layout:**
- Top: Product label — `SARAMONIC AIR & AIR SE`
- Center: The hero number — `90 dB` displayed large and prominent, with competitor comparison below it
- Below center: Product shot of Saramonic Air and/or Air SE
- Bottom: Short closing statement

**On-image copy — exact copy, do not change:**
- Hero number: `90 dB SNR`
- Comparison line: `Kompetitor: 70–75 dB`
- Closing: `Beda 15–20 dB = beda yang kamu dengar.`

**Visual notes:**
- `90 dB` must be the single largest text element on the slide — this is the hero
- Competitor comparison should be visually subordinate — smaller, lower contrast — but still readable
- Product shot: clean, minimal background — Air and Air SE together if possible
- This slide should feel like a confident reveal, not a comparison ad — the number speaks for itself
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

> ⚠️ **Data verified:** 90 dB SNR is a confirmed Saramonic Air & Air SE spec. Competitor range 70–75 dB is brand-confirmed positioning data. Do not modify these numbers.
